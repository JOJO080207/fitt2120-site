Infrared Sauna Benefits

Detoxification

Sauna sessions increase sweating, which helps your body excrete water-soluble byproducts through the skin. Keep fluids and electrolytes up before and after.

Weight Loss

Heat exposure can raise heart rate and energy use similar to light cardio. It’s not a substitute for nutrition and training, but it can complement an overall plan.

Muscle Recovery

Warmth helps reduce perceived muscle soreness and stiffness. Pair your sauna with Post-Sauna Reset for mobility and recovery.

Sleep

Many users find evening sauna sessions help the body wind down and support restful sleep. Cool down before bed for best results.

Anti-Aging / Skin

Consistent heat exposure supports circulation and may promote skin health, helping you look and feel refreshed over time.

Heart Health

Infrared sauna sessions can gently elevate heart rate and support cardiovascular conditioning. Consult a healthcare professional if you have heart concerns.

Immunity

Short, consistent sauna use can stimulate mild heat stress, supporting immune function when paired with good recovery habits.

Relaxation

The warmth promotes calmness and stress relief — a great mental reset after a long day or tough workout.

Wellness information only — not medical advice. Avoid sauna use if you’re heat-sensitive, pregnant, have cardiovascular conditions, or take medications that affect sweating unless cleared by a clinician. Exit immediately if you feel dizzy or unwell.

Safe use & quick tips

  • Hydrate before and after every session.
  • Start with 10–15 minutes; increase gradually.
  • Use a towel barrier between skin and bench.
  • Allow time to cool down after your session.
  • Clean the area when finished — supplies provided.
